## Short evidence notes

Search quality was mixed: broad portal/search-engine queries were noisy, while Domiporta exposed several direct listing pages. Otodom/OLX/Adresowo were not reliably extractable in the available runtime, so I did not loop on blocked or low-yield portal pages.

**Rzecko:** price is unusually low for 1.25 ha with buildings and media stated as water, sewerage, electricity and heating. The red flags are serious: sale is of perpetual usufruct, the transformer is owned by ENEA under a land lease, one residential plot inside the fence is explicitly not included, and the listing says no connection to the new sewage network despite media being listed.

**Koryta:** the scale and A2 location give optionality for workshop/storage, land banking or a semi-rural base. Red flags: perpetual usufruct plus ownership of buildings rather than clean full ownership; technical/magazine buildings may be liabilities; road is ground access from asphalt; MPZP, KW, contamination and utility status need checking.

**Uherce Mineralne:** this is not a cheap rural homestead, but it has a clear retreat/business angle: 9,500 m², two year-round houses, SPA/wellness infrastructure and Bieszczady tourism demand. Only pursue if financials, building permits, occupancy history, maintenance costs, sewage/water and local planning are documented.

## Best next calls

Ask each seller for KW number, exact legal title, MPZP/WZ excerpt, cadastral parcel list, utility connection documents, access-road status, building inventory, known easements/leases, and any environmental or contamination history. For Rzecko and Koryta, negotiate hard around legal complexity and infrastructure/building risk before spending viewing time.
