## Evidence and buyer-side notes

Searches checked included focused Polish queries for siedlisko/land in Lubuskie, Zachodniopomorskie, Dolnośląskie and Bieszczady, plus direct openly accessible Adresowo category/listing pages. Search-engine result quality was weak, so the credible leads above come from direct listing pages with extractable title/description/price metadata.

The **Szprotawa forest parcel** is the asymmetric lead on price and land size, but it is also the easiest to misunderstand: forest land can be a trap if Konrad expects building, clearing, cabins, or flexible private use. First checks: KW ownership, road access type, whether it is actually las in ewidencja, any Natura/protection constraints, and whether the seller has any official planning/building information.

The **Zielątkowo and Sarbinowo building plots** are cleaner administratively on paper because the listings call them budowlana and state asphalt access/water, but they are village plots, not retreat-scale land. Ask for MPZP/WZ excerpt, media locations, road status, dimensions, neighbouring uses, and whether the listed water means a connection or only network nearby.

The **Dzietrzychowice agricultural plot** is less attractive at ~580k PLN/ha and with unconnected utilities. Treat it as negotiation/watch-only unless the seller proves residential WZ, existing siedlisko context, or some location advantage not visible from the listing metadata.
